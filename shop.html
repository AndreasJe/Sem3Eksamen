0-2 years old

For the first year, your baby thrives by observing their environment. Their vision is still not the best, so toys with contrasting colours are their favourite.
It is a great idea to have toys that have different textures and can make different sounds. Wooden toys that rattle are enjoyed enormously by a baby, as their hearing is one of the senses they are more close to. As their senses evolve, they will start paying attention to new aspects of the same toys. What at first was only a sound will start having an origin and a reason. Let your loved one learn about basic shapes and object relationships.

Towards their second year of age, they are acquiring rough and fine motor skills. At this point they love stacking things, matching shapes and making noise. The same rattling toys they loved as babies can now become tambourines. At this point, simple 3-4 pieces puzzles are also a good idea to introduce in their world.

Most toys for this age group are already assembled or just need very simple assembling.

3-4 years old

In this stage of their lives, your toddlers love playing with a purpose. They are learning about life by imitating the real world. They want to do what they see you do, so they can help you while you are cooking, and they can have equipment that mimics real tools, such as knives, wrenches and others.

Their fine motor skills have fully developed, so they can now build with blocks and complete puzzles all by themselves. It is a good stage for your children to learn about building things, they will enjoy building the toys with you, and they will want to be part of the process and feel useful.

Now, they are ready to learn about shapes, colours, numbers and letters. They will still enjoy toys that require sorting shapes or colours, and they will be thrilled to be able to name the colours and shapes. Their energy levels are high, and they love pushing and pulling things. Animals with wheels, trucks, and even trains are a great way to let them play and learn cause-effect.

Assembling toys together for this age group will be a fun activity. No extra tools needed, as we designed screws made of wood that will fit perfectly from one piece of the toy to the next.

You will notice that your child is more independent now. At this stage, they like to play make-believe. They will see themselves as a character in their play-time, and they will imagine they are pilots, doctors and an infinity of other jobs. They enjoy telling stories through play, and they will love having characters that interact with each other and with the environment.

It is a perfect time to take advantage of their learning abilities, at this point your child will be able to start learning math skills and polish their language skills. They will practice this skill by having their toys have long conversations with each other.

Building things is a huge part of this age group, and they are very imaginative and thorough about it. Blocks, work tools, magnetic toys are very interesting.

Assembling toys together for this age group will be a great bonding activity with extra space to learn. No extra tools needed, as we designed screws made of wood that will fit perfectly from one piece of the toy to the next. Children this age will also be able to take the toys apart and build them back up.
